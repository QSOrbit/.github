Security policy
Thanks for taking the time to look at QSOrbit's security. Even in pre-alpha, we want issues caught early — please reach out before opening a public issue.
Reporting a vulnerability
Email security reports to philgelineau@gmail.com.
Please include enough detail to reproduce the issue — what you tried, what happened, what you expected, and the QSOrbit version (or commit hash) and platform you're on. A proof-of-concept is appreciated but not required.
You should hear back within a few days. QSOrbit is currently maintained by one person, so response times are best-effort — if you don't get a reply within a week, feel free to send a polite nudge.
Disclosure
Please hold off on public disclosure until we've had a chance to investigate and ship a fix. We won't make you wait indefinitely — once a fix is available, or once we've agreed there's no practical path to one, we'll coordinate timing with you.
If you'd like to be credited in the release notes when the fix lands, mention it in your report.
Scope
Anything that affects the safety, integrity, or privacy of QSOrbit users is in scope. If you're unsure whether something counts as security-relevant, send it anyway — we'd rather hear about it and decide together.
Non-security issues
For ordinary bugs, please use GitHub Issues. For questions or feature ideas, Discussions is the right place.
